Important Risk Warning

Trading foreign exchange (Forex) and other leveraged financial instruments carries a high level of risk and may not be suitable for all investors. Before deciding to trade, you should carefully consider your investment objectives, level of experience, and risk appetite.

📉

High Risk of Loss

Forex trading involves substantial risk of loss and is not appropriate for all investors. The high degree of leverage available in Forex trading can work against you as well as for you. The possibility exists that you could sustain a loss of some or all of your initial investment.

You should never trade with money you cannot afford to lose. Only surplus funds should be placed at risk in the Forex market.

  • Losses can exceed your initial deposit and margin requirements
  • Market volatility can result in rapid and significant losses
  • Emotional trading decisions often amplify losses
  • No trading strategy guarantees profits or eliminates risk
⚡

Leverage and Margin Risks

Leverage allows traders to control large positions with a relatively small amount of capital. While leverage can magnify profits, it equally magnifies losses. Trading on margin means you are borrowing funds to increase your position size.

A small market movement against your position can result in a margin call or automatic liquidation of your positions, potentially resulting in total loss of your margin deposit.

  • Higher leverage increases both potential returns and potential losses
  • Margin calls may require additional funds on short notice
  • Stop-loss orders may not limit losses in volatile markets
  • Gap risk exists when markets open at significantly different prices
🌍

Market and Liquidity Risks

Forex markets are influenced by numerous factors including economic indicators, geopolitical events, central bank policies, and market sentiment. These factors can cause rapid and unpredictable price movements.

  • News releases can cause extreme volatility and slippage
  • Low liquidity periods (weekends, holidays) may widen spreads
  • Interbank market conditions affect retail pricing and execution
  • Currency correlations can amplify portfolio risk unexpectedly

Market conditions during major economic announcements can result in execution at prices significantly different from expected entry or exit points.

Technical and Execution Risks

Online trading relies on technology infrastructure including internet connectivity, trading platforms, and broker systems. Technical failures can prevent order execution or cause delays.

  • Internet connectivity issues may prevent order placement or modification
  • Platform outages during critical market moments can result in losses
  • Slippage occurs when orders execute at prices different from requested prices
  • Latency in data feeds may affect real-time decision making
  • Cybersecurity threats pose risks to account access and funds

Psychological and Emotional Risks

Trading can be emotionally and psychologically demanding. Fear, greed, and stress can impair judgment and lead to poor decision-making. The psychological impact of financial losses should not be underestimated.

  • Revenge trading after losses often compounds problems
  • Overconfidence after wins can lead to excessive risk-taking
  • Trading addiction is a recognized behavioral risk
  • Financial stress can affect personal relationships and mental health
